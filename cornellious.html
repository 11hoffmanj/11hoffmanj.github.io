The Project

Cornellious is a specialized humor site aimed at Cornell University students. Launched in January, 2013, Cornellious has matured from a pet project of Max Martinez and myself into a beloved humor hub for the campus. Our goal was twofold: make students laugh and give back to the community. We've turned revenue from Cornellious apparel into over $3000 in donations for charities in the Ithaca area. In our best month, April of 2013, we had over 6,000 unique visitors and nearly half a million hits.

My Role

I designed and developed the Cornellious Wordpress theme, illustrated custom interaction buttons, and created graphics for the site and branding. I also collaborated closely with the founder to grow Cornellious into a staple on campus.

A Place for Cornellians to Laugh

The design of Cornellious is meant to be a kitchy and comedic take on Cornell itself, complete with Ivy League reds and an overly dramatic typeface. Max wanted the site to feel young, so we brought in dotted lines to recall grade school handwriting lessons, and backwards C's as a juvenile bastardation of Cornell's everpresent C.

After sketching out initial plans, I created two photoshop mockups before moving to CSS, HTML, and PHP.

We put a splash page up that would mirror the satirical, euphoric vision of Cornellious.
