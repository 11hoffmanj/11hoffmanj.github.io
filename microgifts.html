The Project

Microgifts was an iPhone app and 501(c)3 non-profit established to provide an efficient mobile platform for the collection and distribution of money to charities. Developed in 2010, Microgifts was an early entrant into the scene of crowd-funding. With a donation as little as $0.99, an iPhone user joins an organic – and virtually infinite – community of charitable microgivers, and brings to life the mission of the app's creators: to enable a vast audience to participate in the giving process and to be part of something much larger than themselves. After three years, and elongated grappling with Apple over certain in-App donation allowances, we shut down Microgifts.

My Role

I co-founded Microgifts with two classmates during my junior year of high school. I led the UX/UI for the App, designed and coded a fully Flash website, and worked largely on growing Microgifts as a non-profit. We partnered with over a dozen reputable charities, honed our business plan, and made waves within the charity sphere at a young age.

The app

How do we make giving fun and functional at the same time? The Microgifts user experience is driven by these coexisting thoughts. We offer easy and clean navigation, and emphasize the wonderful endorphins of giving.
